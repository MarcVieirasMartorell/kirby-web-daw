Kirby's run through the years

Kirby's Dream Land (1992)

Kirby's first adventure on the Game Boy. No copy abilities yet, but a charming start!

Kirby's Adventure (1993)

Introduced Kirby's iconic copy abilities, changing the series forever.

Kirby's Dream Land 2 (1995)

Introduced animal friends that enhanced Kirby's abilities.

Kirby Super Star (1996)

A collection of mini-games and adventures, introducing partner mechanics.

Kirby's Dream Land 3 (1997)

Beautiful pastel-style graphics and more animal friends.

Kirby 64: The Crystal Shards (2000)

First 3D-styled Kirby game, introduced ability combinations.

Kirby Air Ride (2003)

A fast-paced racing game starring Kirby and friends.

Kirby & The Amazing Mirror (2004)

Metroidvania-style gameplay with multiplayer mechanics.

Kirby: Canvas Curse (2005)

Innovative stylus-based controls on the Nintendo DS.

Kirby: Squeak Squad (2006)

Traditional 2D Kirby platforming with a treasure-hunting twist.

Kirby's Epic Yarn (2010)

Unique fabric-based visuals with a relaxing difficulty.

Kirby's Return to Dream Land (2011)

Classic 2D gameplay with cooperative multiplayer.

Kirby: Triple Deluxe (2014)

Introduced the Hypernova ability and 3D layered gameplay.

Kirby: Planet Robobot (2016)

Added mech suits for Kirby, blending tech with traditional gameplay.

Kirby Star Allies (2018)

Introduced friend mechanics, allowing enemies to join Kirby.

Kirby and the Forgotten Land (2022)

Kirby's first full 3D platformer, featuring Mouthful Mode.

About Kirby's copy ability

Copy Ability is a catch-all term referring to Kirby's trademark ability to inherit and utilize the special powers and/or skills of the entities he swallows using his inhale. First introduced in Kirby's Adventure, this ability grants the pink puffball a potentially massive array of different methods of attack and means of traversing the lands he finds himself in. Starting with games like Kirby Super Star, acquiring a Copy Ability also bestows a unique hat, outfit, or other distinguishing feature upon Kirby, which serves as a quick-glance way to determine what kind of power he has obtained.
